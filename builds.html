Before you start-First, make sure you have parts that will work with the new build. Go to the website https://pcpartpicker.com/ to do this.

Anti-Static

The place where the PC will be built must have a strong link to the ground. There can't be any carpet

Unbox Pc Case

Find where the psu (power supply unit) is located. Put the power supply in place. There should be some screw holes on the back of the case, where the power supply is. Use these screws to keep the power supply in place.

Motherboard

Align all the screws and then put the motherboard into the PC case.

CPU

Now, look at the arrow on the CPU and make sure it matches the arrow on the CPU socket on the motherboard. Also, don't take off the black cover. After putting in the CPU. Use the little button on the right to close it. The black plate will pop up once it's closed.

RAM

Check the instructions for your motherboard to find out where to put your RAM. Mine was on a2 and b2 ram placement. Insert ram until you hear to click.

M.2 Storage

Install m.2 storage or an SSD. Hard drives don't last very long, so we wouldn't suggest them.

24 pin

Connect the 24-pin power wire to the motherboard,Depending on your motherboard, the top left of the board will have either a 4-pin or an 8-pin CPU wire. My motherboard had both an 8-pin and an 8-pin wire for the CPU. The extra 8-pin cable is for overclocking.

GPU

Install the graphics processing unit on the pcie port on the motherboard until you hear a click. Then connect the power supply line to it

F-Panel

Connect the F panel wire from your computer case to the motherboard. This will power the case itself.

Fans

Make sure there is good air flow in the case, place the fans so that they allow the most air to flow, and then connect them to the motherboard,Choosing the right cooler is the last step. Air coolers are a simple and inexpensive way to cool your CPU. I chose an AIO cooler for my build because I liked the way it looked

Aio cooler

16) For the AIO cooler, make sure to remove the old CPU mount bracket from your motherboard and replace it with the new CPU mount bracket that came with your motherboard. Take off the sticker or plastic from your motherboard. It should already have thermal paste on it. Attach the AIO to the CPU and fix it so air can't get out. Connect the wire for the AIO fan to the motherboard, which is located on the top right at the motherboard.

Radiator

The radiator on the AIO cooler can be put on top of the PC case or in the middle of the right side. The best air flow in my PC case is on the middle right side
